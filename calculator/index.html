Where does your rent go?

An estimate of how a building's rent splits between property taxes, the landlord's mortgage, upkeep, and profit. Adjust the numbers to match your building.

Assumptions (every number is a guess you can change)

This is an estimate, not an audit. Property tax is grounded in the building's value, but rent, the mortgage, and operating costs are modeled — change them and the answer moves. Profit can come out negative for a recently-bought or over-leveraged landlord; that's a real result. Built for tenant organizing and teaching, in the spirit of the Southside Together activity that inspired it. Part of rentrate.
